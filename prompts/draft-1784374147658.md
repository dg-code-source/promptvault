**Summarize this YouTube video in exactly  {Bullets:100} bullet points, organized under category headings.**

Rules:
- **Group the {Bullets:100} bullets under category headings** based on the video's natural topics or sections. No limit on the number of headings — use as many as the video's content genuinely supports.
- **Within each category**, bullets must read as one continuous flow, not isolated facts — each bullet should connect logically to the one before it using natural linking language (e.g., "this leads to," "because of this," "next," "as a result"). A new heading can start a fresh thread.
- **Bold** the 1-2 most important words or key phrase in each bullet (the term or idea a skimmer should catch even if reading quickly).
- Follow the video's own structure and reasoning when ordering categories — if it's chronological, follow that order; if it builds a non-linear argument, order categories by logical flow instead.
- Optimize for **quick skimming**: someone reading only the bolded words and headings should get the gist of the entire video.
- Distribute the  {Bullets:100} bullets across headings roughly in proportion to how much time/depth the video spends on each topic — don't force equal counts per heading.
- No intro, outro, or meta-commentary — just the headings and bullets.
